POWERHOUSE PATNA GYM

HARSH RAJ WORKOUT PLAN

MONDAY

Back & Biceps · Primary Pull

Width + thickness + arm curl volume + forearm finisher
01 T-Bar Bent-Over Row
3 × 12–16
  • Hip hinge ~45°; chest up, back flat throughout
  • Pull handle to lower sternum; elbows stay close
  • Squeeze shoulder blades at the top — hold 1 sec
  • Full stretch at bottom; don't round upper back under load
Pro Tip: This is your primary back thickness builder. The hip-hinge position mirrors a takedown stance. Load progressively — add 2.5 kg when you hit the top range easily.
02 Lat Pulldown
3 × 12–16
  • Lean back 15°; initiate by depressing scapula first
  • Pull bar to upper sternum — not behind the neck
  • Full stretch at top every rep; let shoulder blades rise
  • Thumbless grip removes forearm interference
Pro Tip: Think "pull your elbows to your pockets" not "pull the bar down." This mental cue shifts the load from biceps to lats instantly.
03 Seated Row
3 × 14–20
  • Slight forward lean at start for full lat stretch
  • Pull to upper abdomen; elbows hug torso
  • Squeeze blades at peak; slow return to full stretch
Pro Tip: Higher rep range (14–20) here creates metabolic stress for back density. The constant cable tension is a different stimulus than free-weight rows.
04 Pullover
2 × Failure
  • Lie across bench; hips low to maximise lat stretch
  • Arms straight (soft elbow bend); arc from overhead to hips
  • Feel the lat stretch at full extension — don't rush it
  • Go to true muscular failure; last set leave nothing behind
Pro Tip: Pullovers target the lats through a unique arc. They also develop the serratus anterior — visible as the "fingers" at the side of the chest.
05 Preacher Curl
3 × 12–16
  • Chest flat against pad; upper arms fully supported
  • Full extension at the bottom — don't cut the range
  • Slow curl; hard squeeze at top; use EZ-bar for comfort
Pro Tip: The pad eliminates momentum entirely. When you can't cheat, every rep is pure muscle work.
06 Dumbbell Curl
3 × Failure
  • Alternate or simultaneous — keep elbows pinned to sides
  • Supinate (twist pinky out) during the curl for peak contraction
  • Go to failure; last set should be difficult by rep 10+
  • No swinging — if you're swinging, the set is over
Pro Tip: Going to failure here after preacher curls = double the stimulus. This is a classic high-volume bicep protocol.
07 Reverse Grip Curls
3 × 12
  • Overhand (pronated) grip; elbows fixed at sides
  • Full ROM: complete extension to full contraction
  • Lighter than regular curls — control the weight
Pro Tip: Builds the brachialis which pushes the bicep up. Also develops grip endurance critical for pulling sports.
08 Reverse Wrist Curls
3 × Failure
  • Forearms on thighs, backs of hands facing up
  • Extend wrists fully upward; use very light weight
  • Extensors are much weaker than flexors — respect the weight
Pro Tip: Training extensors creates balance and protects against tennis elbow.
09 Bar Forearm Pushdown
2 × 15
  • Cable machine, bar attachment at chest height; overhand grip
  • Push bar down by extending wrists; elbows fixed
  • Controlled return to full wrist flexion
Pro Tip: This rare exercise isolates the extensor compartment in a unique way.
10 Reverse Wrist Curls (2nd Hit)
3 × 12
  • Same as above; slightly lighter since pre-fatigued
  • Focus on full range and peak contraction
Note: Second hit on extensors for full development. Forearms recover quickly.
11 Wrist Curls
3 × 12
  • Sit; forearms on thighs, palms up, wrists over knees
  • Let bar roll to fingertips on descent — maximises ROM
  • Curl wrists up as high as possible; hold briefly
Pro Tip: The "roll to fingertips" doubles the range of motion. This is where growth stimulus lives.

TUESDAY

Chest & Triceps · Primary Push

Heavy incline → volume incline → fly isolation → flat → decline → tri burnout
01 Incline DB Press — Heavy
3 × 6–12
  • 30–45° bench; pinch shoulder blades before unracking
  • Lower DBs to outer chest; elbows ~75° from torso
  • Drive through palms; rotate slightly inward at top
  • Heaviest set of the workout — go here fresh
Pro Tip: This is your strength set — aim for 6-8 reps with a weight that's genuinely challenging. The heavy incline DB sets the upper-chest foundation.
02 Incline Bench — Slow (40–50 kg)
1 × 16–20
  • Moderate weight; 3-sec eccentric, 1-sec pause at bottom
  • Touch chest; full extension at top
  • The slow tempo is intentional — fight every rep
Why This Works: Back-to-back incline creates both mechanical tension and metabolic stress. Two different growth signals.
03 Pec Deck Fly
3 × 18
  • Sit tall; forearms on pads or hands on handles
  • Bring pads together with a "hugging" motion; hold 1 sec
  • Control the return fully — that stretch is the stimulus
  • 18 reps: go moderate weight, full range priority
Pro Tip: Pec deck isolates the chest better because there's no tricep involvement. The stretch is where most pec damage (and growth) occurs.
04 Flat Bench Press
2 × 6–12
  • Natural arch; feet drive into floor; leg drive = stability
  • Touch bar to lower sternum; elbows 45–60° to torso
  • "Pull the bar apart" to activate lats and protect shoulders
Note: Only 2 sets because you've already pre-fatigued the chest. These sets will feel harder than usual.
05 Decline DB Press
3 × 16
  • Decline bench; DBs at chest level, elbows wide
  • Press up and slightly inward; squeeze hard at top
  • 16 reps = moderate weight, pump focus
Pro Tip: Lower chest fibres only activate fully from a decline angle. This set fills the gap most people skip.
06 Triceps Pushdown
3 × Failure
  • Rope or bar; slight forward hinge; elbows pinned to sides
  • Split rope at bottom for full lateral-head squeeze
  • Let rope rise to chin level for complete stretch
Pro Tip: Going to failure after chest means triceps are already fatigued. You'll reach failure faster — that's expected.
07 Overhead Extension
3 × Failure
  • Face away from cable; rope overhead, arms bent
  • Extend arms forward fully; hold squeeze at full extension
  • Return slowly — the stretch is the money position
Pro Tip: The only way to fully stretch the long head — the largest of the three heads. Pairing this with pushdown = complete tricep stimulus.
08 Crunches
3 × 15–20
  • Hands at temples; curl ribs to hips; don't pull neck
  • Focus on full contraction of the upper abs
09 Leg Raises
3 × 12–15
  • Posterior pelvic tilt at top activates lower abs
  • Keep legs straight or slightly bent; control descent
10 Plank
3 × 30–60s
  • Rigid body; squeeze glutes + quads + abs simultaneously
  • Maintain a flat back throughout

WEDNESDAY

Shoulders · 3D Delts

Press → lateral isolation → rear delt → traps → forearm finisher
01 DB Press
3 × 8–14
  • Seated or standing; DBs at ear level, palms forward
  • Press up and slightly inward; full extension overhead
  • Control descent — don't drop below ear level
  • 8–14 range: heavier sets early, lighter as fatigue builds
Pro Tip: DB press allows each arm to move independently, fixing strength imbalances. If one arm is noticeably weaker, match reps from that side.
02 Cable Lateral Raise
3 × 16–20
  • Cable at ankle height; lean slightly away from stack
  • Lead with elbow; tilt pinky up slightly at shoulder height
  • Controlled descent — 2 sec down minimum
  • 16–20 reps: this is a pump exercise, not a strength exercise
Cable vs Dumbbell: Cable provides constant tension through the full range. It's objectively more effective for medial delt isolation.
03 Reverse Pec Deck — Slow
3 × Failure
  • Face the pec deck; arms forward, slight elbow bend
  • Drive elbows back and out in a wide arc
  • "Slow" is key — 3-sec eccentric; don't use traps to assist
  • Hold 1 sec at rear peak; feel the rear delt squeeze
Critical: Rear delts are essential for shoulder health, posture, and the 3D "capped" shoulder look. The slow tempo doubles the stimulus.
04 Cable Shrugs
3 × 12
  • Cable at low position; straight arms; shrug straight up
  • Hold at peak for 2 sec — don't roll the shoulders forward
  • Controlled descent; full range
Pro Tip: Cable shrugs keep constant tension vs barbell. The upward shrug path keeps movement in trap fibres, not rotator cuff.
05 Reverse Grip Curls
3 × 12
  • Overhand grip; elbows fixed at sides
  • Controlled motion; full expansion
06 Reverse Wrist Curls
3 × Failure
  • Forearms on thighs, back of hands up
  • Extend wrists upward fully
07 Bar Forearm Pushdown
2 × 15
  • Cable machine; overhand grip; extend wrists down
08 Wrist Curls
3 × 12
  • Palms up; roll to fingertips on descent

THURSDAY

Legs · Lower Body Strength

Heavy compound squats → quad isolation → hamstring isolation → abs
01 Heavy Squats
3 × 8–14
  • Bar on mid-trap shelf; brace core hard before every rep
  • Break at hips and knees simultaneously; descend to parallel+
  • Knees track over toes; drive through full foot
  • Chest stays tall; don't good-morning it up
  • Build to bodyweight squat by end of 3 months
Pro Tip: The king of lower-body exercises. Every rep strengthens quads, glutes, core, and stabilisers simultaneously. Track your numbers weekly.
02 Leg Extension
3 × 20
  • Align knee joint with machine's cam pivot point
  • Extend fully and squeeze the VMO at the top
  • 3-sec eccentric every rep; toes slightly inward for inner quad
  • 20 reps = moderate weight; metabolic pump focus
Pro Tip: 20-rep leg extensions after heavy squats create brutal quad pump and metabolic stress. Together, they're comprehensive.
03 Leg Curl
3 × 20
  • Hips flat on pad; curl fully until heels near glutes
  • Hold peak contraction 1 sec; 3-sec eccentric
  • Pointed toes increases peak hamstring contraction
Why 20 Reps: Hamstrings respond extremely well to high reps. This balances the leg work and protects the knee joint.
04 Crunches
3 × 15–20
  • Focus on rib-to-hip contraction — not pulling the neck
05 Leg Raises
3 × 12–15
  • Curl hips up at the top for lower ab activation
06 Plank
3 × 30–60s
  • Progress: when 60s feels easy, add a weight plate on your back

FRIDAY

Back & Biceps Hit 2 · Pull Volume

Deadlifts → volume rows → isolation curls → forearm finisher
01 Deadlifts — Moderate
3 × 8–12
  • Bar over mid-foot; shins touch bar; back flat
  • "Push the floor away" to initiate; squeeze glutes at top
  • Reset every rep — don't bounce off the floor
  • 8–12 range: focus on perfect form over max weight
Why Moderate? You've trained back already this week. This is for posterior chain reinforcement and structural integrity, not a 1RM attempt.
02 One-Arm DB Row
3 × 12–16
  • Support one hand on bench; torso parallel to floor
  • Pull DB to hip — not to the chest; arc the weight
  • Full stretch at bottom; don't rotate the torso
Pro Tip: Unilateral (one-arm) work ensures both sides of your back develop equally. Pulling to the hip maximises lower lat engagement.
03 Lat Pulldown — Wide
3 × 14–18
  • Wide grip; focus on outer lat "flaring"
  • Pull to chin height; controlled return
Pro Tip: Higher reps (14–18) for the second pull session of the week builds endurance and density.
04 Seated Row — Neutral
3 × 14–20
  • Neutral (palms facing) grip; pull to belly button
  • Chest up; squeeze shoulder blades hard
05 Face Pulls
3 × 20
  • Rope to forehead; pull handles apart at peak
  • Focus on rear delts and mid-traps
Shoulder Health: Face pulls counteract the "slumped" posture and strengthen the rotator cuff. Non-negotiable for longevity.
06 Hammer Curls
3 × 12–16
  • Neutral grip (palms in); keep elbows fixed
  • Develops the brachialis and brachioradialis (forearm)
Pro Tip: Hammer curls make the arms look thicker from the front. Essential for that "wide" arm look.
07 Concentration Curl
3 × 12
  • Seated; elbow against inner thigh; curl DB up
  • No momentum possible; pure bicep peak work
Pro Tip: Focus entirely on the squeeze. Imagine the bicep peaking as you reach the top.
08 Bicep Curl — DB/Bar
2 × Failure
  • Strict form; go until you can't complete a full rep
09 Reverse Grip Curls
3 × 12
  • Focus on the "top" part of the forearm.
10 Reverse Wrist Curls
3 × Failure
  • Burn them out.
11 Bar Forearm Pushdown
2 × 15
  • Control the negative.
12 Reverse Wrist Curls (Repeat)
3 × 12
  • Final metabolic push.
13 Wrist Curls
3 × 12
  • Max ROM.
