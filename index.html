HARSH RAJ WORKOUT PLAN

MONDAY

Back & Biceps · Primary Pull

01 T-Bar Bent-Over Row
3 × 12–16
  • Hip hinge ~45°; chest up, back flat throughout
  • Pull handle to lower sternum; elbows stay close
  • Squeeze shoulder blades at the top — hold 1 sec
  • Full stretch at bottom; don't round upper back
Your primary back thickness builder. Load progressively — add 2.5 kg when you hit the top range easily.
02 Lat Pulldown
3 × 12–16
  • Lean back 15°; initiate by depressing scapula first
  • Pull bar to upper sternum — not behind the neck
  • Full stretch at top every rep; let shoulder blades rise
Think "pull your elbows to your pockets" not "pull the bar down." This shifts load from biceps to lats.
03 Seated Row
3 × 14–20
  • Slight forward lean at start for full lat stretch
  • Pull to upper abdomen; elbows hug torso
  • Squeeze blades at peak; slow return
Higher rep range here creates metabolic stress for back density. Constant cable tension is key.
04 Pullover
2 × Failure
  • Lie across bench; hips low to maximise lat stretch
  • Arms straight; arc from overhead to hips
  • Feel the lat stretch at full extension
Targets the lats through a unique arc. Also develops the serratus anterior — the "fingers" at the side.
05 Preacher Curl
3 × 12–16
  • Chest flat against pad; upper arms fully supported
  • Full extension at the bottom — don't cut the range
  • Slow curl; hard squeeze at top
The pad eliminates momentum entirely. When you can't cheat, every rep is pure muscle work.
06 Dumbbell Curl
3 × Failure
  • Keep elbows pinned to sides
  • Supinate (twist pinky out) during the curl
  • No swinging — if you swing, the set is over
Going to failure here after preacher curls = maximum growth stimulus.
07 Reverse Grip Curls
3 × 12
  • Overhand grip; elbows fixed at sides
  • Lighter than regular curls — control is key
Builds the brachialis which pushes the bicep up, creating arm thickness.
08 Wrist Curls
3 × 12
  • Forearms on thighs, palms up
  • Let bar roll to fingertips on descent
The "roll to fingertips" doubles the range of motion. This is where growth lives.

TUESDAY

Chest & Triceps · Primary Push

01 Incline DB Press — Heavy
3 × 6–12
  • 30–45° bench; pinch shoulder blades before unracking
  • Lower DBs to outer chest; elbows ~75° from torso
  • Drive through palms; rotate slightly inward at top
Your strength set — aim for 6-8 reps with a weight that's genuinely challenging.
02 Incline Bench — Slow
1 × 16–20
  • Moderate weight (40-50kg); 3-sec eccentric, 1-sec pause at bottom
  • Touch chest; full extension at top
Back-to-back incline creates both mechanical tension and metabolic stress.
03 Pec Deck Fly
3 × 18
  • Bring pads together with a "hugging" motion; hold 1 sec
  • Control the return fully — that stretch is the stimulus
Pec deck isolates the chest better than almost anything else.
04 Flat Bench Press
2 × 6–12
  • Natural arch; feet drive into floor; leg drive = stability
  • Touch bar to lower sternum; elbows 45–60° to torso
Only 2 sets because you've already pre-fatigued the chest.
05 Decline DB Press
3 × 16
  • Press up and slightly inward; squeeze hard at top
  • 16 reps = moderate weight, pump focus
Lower chest fibres only activate fully when pressing from a decline angle.
06 Triceps Pushdown
3 × Failure
  • Rope or bar; elbows pinned to sides
  • Split rope at bottom for full lateral-head squeeze
Going to failure after chest means your triceps are already partially fatigued.
07 Overhead Extension
3 × Failure
  • Rope overhead; arms forward fully; hold squeeze
  • Return slowly — the stretch is the money position
The only way to fully stretch the long head — the largest head.

WEDNESDAY

Shoulders · 3D Delts

01 DB Press
3 × 8–14
  • Seated or standing; DBs at ear level
  • Press up and slightly inward; full extension
Allows each arm to move independently, fixing strength imbalances.
02 Cable Lateral Raise
3 × 16–20
  • Lead with elbow; tilt pinky up slightly at top
  • Controlled descent — 2 sec down minimum
Cable provides constant tension through the full range.
03 Reverse Pec Deck — Slow
3 × Failure
  • 3-sec eccentric; don't use traps to assist
  • Hold 1 sec at rear peak; feel the rear delt squeeze
Rear delts are essential for the 3D "capped" shoulder look.
04 Cable Shrugs
3 × 12
  • Straight arms; shrug straight up; hold for 2 sec
  • Don't roll the shoulders forward
Constant tension vs barbell which loses it at the bottom.

THURSDAY

Legs · Lower Body Strength

01 Heavy Squats
3 × 8–14
  • Brace core hard; descend to parallel+
  • Knees track over toes; chest stays tall
The king of lower-body exercises. Track your numbers weekly.
02 Leg Extension
3 × 20
  • Squeeze the VMO at the top; 3-sec eccentric
  • 20 reps = moderate weight, pump focus
Brutal pump after squats — a different stimulus.
03 Leg Curl
3 × 20
  • Hold peak contraction 1 sec; 3-sec eccentric
  • Pointed toes for max hamstring contraction
Hamstrings respond extremely well to high reps.

01 Reverse Grip Row
3 × 12–16

  • Emphasises lower lats more than overhand
Shifts load to lower lats and involves biceps more heavily.
02 Lat Pulldown (Alt Grip)
3 × 12–16
  • Use a different grip than Monday (Wide/Neutral/Under)
Rotating grip weekly ensures you develop full lat coverage.
03 Seated Row
3 × 14–20
Second hit builds strength faster. Consistency is key.
04 Hammer Curl
3 × Failure
  • Neutral grip (thumbs up); no swinging
Focuses on the brachialis for arm thickness.
