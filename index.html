POWERHOUSE PATNA GYM

HARSH RAJ WORKOUT PLAN

MONDAY

Back & Biceps - Primary Pull

Width + thickness + arm curl volume + forearm finisher
Back - Thickness & Width ▼
01 T-Bar Row
3 x 12–16
  • Hip hinge ~45°; chest up, back flat throughout
  • Pull handle to lower sternum; elbows stay close
  • Squeeze shoulder blades at the top - hold 1 sec
  • Full stretch at bottom; don't round upper back
Pro Tip: Primary thickness builder. The hip-hinge position mirrors a takedown stance.
02 Lat Pulldown
3 x 12–16
  • Lean back 15°; initiate by depressing scapula
  • Pull bar to upper sternum - not behind the neck
  • Full stretch at top; let shoulder blades rise
Pro Tip: Think "pull elbows to pockets" to shift load from biceps to lats instantly.
03 Seated Row
3 x 14–20
  • Slight forward lean at start for full lat stretch
  • Pull to upper abdomen; elbows hug torso
  • Squeeze blades at peak; slow return
Pro Tip: Higher reps (14-20) here create metabolic stress for back density.
04 Pullover
2 x Failure
  • Lie across bench; hips low to maximise stretch
  • Arms straight (soft bend); arc from overhead to hips
Pro Tip: Develops the serratus anterior - the "fingers" at the side of the chest.
Biceps - Peak & Contraction ▼
05 Preacher Curl
3 x 12–16
  • Chest flat against pad; upper arms supported
  • Full extension at the bottom - don't cut ROM
Pro Tip: Eliminates momentum. When you can't cheat, every rep is pure muscle work.
06 Dumbbell Curl
3 x Failure
  • Supinate (twist pinky out) for peak contraction
  • No swinging - if you're swinging, the set is over
Pro Tip: Going to failure here after preachers maximizes growth stimulus.
Forearms - Grip & Thickness ▼
07 Reverse Grip Curls
3 x 12
  • Overhand grip; builds the brachialis for arm "thickness"
08 Reverse Wrist Curls
3 x Failure
  • Light weight; focus on the burn in the extensors
09 Bar Forearm Pushdown
2 x 15
  • Push bar down by extending wrists; elbows fixed
10 Reverse Wrist Curls (2nd Hit)
3 x 12
  • Slightly lighter; focus on peak contraction
11 Wrist Curls
3 x 12
  • Roll to fingertips; curl up high
Note: Closes the primary pull session.

TUESDAY

Chest & Triceps - Primary Push

Heavy incline - volume incline - fly isolation - flat - decline - tri burnout
Chest - Upper Focus ▼
01 Incline DB Press - Heavy
3 x 6–12
  • 30-45° bench; pinch shoulder blades before unracking
  • Lower DBs to outer chest; elbows ~75° from torso
  • Heaviest set of the workout - go here fresh
Pro Tip: Primary strength set. Aim for 6-8 reps with a weight that's genuinely challenging.
02 Incline Bench - Slow
1 x 16–20
  • Moderate weight; 3-sec eccentric, 1-sec pause at bottom
  • The slow tempo is intentional - fight every rep
Why This Works: Back-to-back incline creates both mechanical tension and metabolic stress.
03 Pec Deck Fly
3 x 18
  • Hugging motion; hold 1 sec at peak contraction
  • Control the return fully - the stretch is the stimulus
Pro Tip: Isolates the chest by removing tricep involvement. The stretch is where growth occurs.
04 Flat Bench Press
2 x 6–12
  • Touch bar to lower sternum; elbows 45-60° to torso
  • "Pull the bar apart" to activate lats and protect shoulders
Note: Pre-fatigued chest makes these 2 sets extremely effective for volume.
05 Decline DB Press
3 x 16
  • Decline angle targets lower fibres; press up and inward
Note: Closes the chest session by hitting the final remaining fibre angle.
Triceps - Isolation ▼
06 Triceps Pushdown
3 x Failure
  • Split rope at bottom; let rope rise to chin for stretch
Pro Tip: Triceps are already fatigued from pressing. Failure will come fast.
07 Cable Overhead Extension
3 x Failure
  • Maximum stretch on the long head; return slowly
Pro Tip: Essential for total tricep development. Leave nothing in the tank.
Abs - Core Stability ▼
08 Crunches
3 x 15–20
  • Hands at temples; curl ribs to hips; don't pull neck
  • Breathe out at the top; feel the squeeze
09 Leg Raises
3 x 12–15
  • Posterior pelvic tilt at top activates lower abs
  • Keep lower back flat against floor
10 Plank
3 x 30–60s
  • Rigid body; squeeze glutes + quads + abs simultaneously
Progress: Beat your previous time by 5 seconds at minimum.

WEDNESDAY

Shoulders - 3D Delts

Press - lateral isolation - rear delt - traps - forearm finisher
Shoulders - Roundness ▼
01 DB Press
3 x 8–14
  • Seated or standing; DBs at ear level, palms forward
  • Press up and slightly inward; full extension overhead
  • Control descent - don't drop below ear level
Pro Tip: Fixing strength imbalances. Match reps from the weaker side first.
02 Cable Lateral Raise
3 x 16–20
  • Cable at ankle height; lead with elbow; tilt pinky up slightly
  • Controlled descent - 2 sec down minimum
Cable vs DB: Constant tension makes this superior for medial delt isolation.
03 Reverse Pec Deck - Slow
3 x Failure
  • Drive elbows back in wide arc; 3-sec eccentric
  • Hold 1 sec at rear peak peak; feel the squeeze
Critical: Essential for shoulder health and the 3D "capped" look.
04 Cable Shrugs
3 x 12
  • Straight arms; shrug straight up; hold for 2 sec
  • Don't roll shoulders; controlled descent
Pro Tip: Upward path keeps movement in traps, not rotator cuff.
Forearms - Isolation ▼
05 Reverse Grip Curls
3 x 12
  • Overhand grip; controlled motion; full expansion
06 Reverse Wrist Curls
3 x Failure
  • Forearms on thighs; extend wrists upward fully
07 Bar Forearm Pushdown
2 x 15
  • Cable machine; overhand grip; extend wrists down
08 Wrist Curls
3 x 12
  • Roll to fingertips; curl wrists up as high as possible
Note: Closes the Wednesday session.

THURSDAY

Legs - Lower Body Strength

Heavy squats - leg press - extensions - curls - calf raises - abs
Legs - Heavy ▼
01 Barbell Squats
3 x 8–14
  • Bar on mid-trap shelf; brace core hard before every rep
  • Break at hips and knees simultaneously; descend to parallel+
  • Knees track over toes; drive through full foot
  • Chest stays tall; don't "good-morning" it up
Pro Tip: The squat is the king of lower-body exercises. Track your squat numbers weekly.
02 Leg Press
3 x 16–20
  • Feet shoulder-width on platform; press up (don't lock knees)
  • Lower weight until knees are at 90°; avoid "butt wink"
  • Focus on the quad push - higher reps for metabolic stress
Note: Higher rep range here builds massive work capacity.
Legs - Isolation ▼
03 Leg Extension
3 x 20
  • Align knee joint with machine's cam pivot point
  • Extend fully and squeeze the VMO at the top
  • 3-sec eccentric every rep; toes slightly inward
Pro Tip: 20-rep leg extensions create brutal quad pump and metabolic stress.
04 Lying Leg Curl
3 x 20
  • Hips flat on pad; curl fully until heels near glutes
  • Hold peak contraction 1 sec; 3-sec eccentric
  • Pointed toes increases peak hamstring contraction
05 Seated Leg Curls
3 x 20
  • Lock the thigh pad tight; sit tall
  • Pull heels under the bench; slow return
  • Maximises hamstring contraction in the shortened position
Pro Tip: Different angles hit different parts of the hamstring complex. Complete development requires both.
Calves - Frequency ▼
06 Standing Calf Raise
3 x 16–20
  • Full stretch at the bottom; pause 1 sec to kill momentum
  • Explode up to toes; hold peak contraction 2 sec
Critical: Most people bounce calf raises. The pause at the bottom is what makes them grow.
07 Seated Calf Raise
3 x 16–20
  • Seated position isolates the soleus (under the gastroc)
  • Full ROM; high reps for endurance and size
Abs - Core Stability ▼
08 Crunches
3 x 15–20
  • Hands at temples; curl ribs to hips; don't pull neck
  • Breathe out at the top; feel the squeeze
09 Leg Raises
3 x 12–15
  • Posterior pelvic tilt at top activates lower abs
  • Keep lower back flat against floor
10 Plank
3 x 30–60s
  • Rigid body; squeeze glutes + quads + abs simultaneously
Progress: Beat your previous time by 5 seconds at minimum.

FRIDAY

Back & Biceps Hit 2 - Pull Volume

Deadlifts - volume rows - isolation curls - forearm finisher
Back - Alternate Angles ▼
01 Reverse Grip Bent-Over Row
3 x 12–16
  • Underhand (supinated) grip; hip hinge same as T-bar row
  • Elbows stay closer to torso than overhand row
  • Pull to lower abdomen; supinated grip emphasises lower lats
Why Different: Monday's row used an overhand grip. Today's reverse grip shifts load to lower lats and involves biceps more.
02 Lat Pulldown
3 x 12–16
  • Use wide overhand OR underhand supinated grip today
  • Different grips recruit different lat fibre regions
Pro Tip: Rotating grip weekly prevents adaptation and ensures full back development.
03 Seated Row
3 x 14–20
  • Same execution as Monday; vary the attachment if available
  • Wide grip = upper-back; close V-bar = lower lat
Note: Second weekly hit on the same movement pattern builds strength faster.
04 Pullover
2 x Failure
  • Same execution as Monday; go slightly heavier or slower
  • Full stretch at overhead position — the key stimulus
Biceps - Hammer Variation ▼
05 Hammer Curl
3 x Failure
  • Neutral grip (thumbs up); elbows fixed at sides
  • Curl in a vertical plane; wrists stay neutral throughout
  • Full extension at bottom; don't swing
Why Different: Monday focused on peak. Hammer curls focus on brachialis to push the bicep up for arm thickness.
Forearms - Isolation Finisher ▼
06 Reverse Grip Curls
3 x 12
  • Pronated grip; slow and controlled to build top of forearm
07 Reverse Wrist Curls
3 x Failure
  • Extend wrists upward fully; high rep burnout
08 Bar Forearm Pushdown
2 x 15
  • Overhand grip on cable bar; extend wrists down
09 Reverse Wrist Curls
3 x 12
  • Second set variation for forearm endurance
10 Wrist Curls
3 x 12
  • Roll to fingertips; curl wrists up; max pump focus
